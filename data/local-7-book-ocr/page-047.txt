Heel and Throat measurements Obtain Heel and Throat measurements using the Machinist Figure Method or from a Miter Chart. Add 3/4" to the Heel measurement and 3/8" to the Throat measurement. Draw a 90 degree angle with the vertical line to the left side. Take the Heel measurement plus 3/4" and divide by 2 and mark this number on the vertical line from the base line. Take the Throat measurement plus 3/8" and divide by 2 and mark this number on the vertical line. Find the exact center of both points and draw a semi-circle from this point as shown in Figure I. Trisect this semi-circle into six equal parts and label as shown. Draw 90 degree lines up from the Base Line to the points on the 1/2 circle. Divide the circumference by twice the number of equally divided parts of the semi-circle and draw the Stretch-out for the gore pattem as in Figure 2. Number all the lines of the stretch-out as shown. Take measurements from the base line to numbered points on the 1/2 circle and transfer to corresponding numbered lines on the Stretch-out. Connect all the points on the stretch-out to form the gore pattern. Add for a lap on one end or the other. Use your center punch to mark where the bead and crimp will start and terminate (usually the 4 points).

Two-Piece 45 Degree Elbow by Rise Information required: Step I. Step 2. Step 3. Step 4. Step 5. Step 6. Step 7. Pipe Call Out Size, Number of Degrees, O.D.I., Rise formula Draw a horizontal line. Determine an Apex point to the right side and from the Apex, mark the Center Line Radius point which is 1 L times the Pipe Call-out size (all 45 degree Ells are considered to be Long Radius). Since this is a 45 degree elbow and a two-piece fitting, each angle is 22 1/2 degrees. The Tangent of the Angle times Center Line Radius Distance equals Rise. Calculate the rise and transfer that measurement to the Center Line. Draw a line from the Apex through the Rise point to a point past the semi-circle. Draw a semi-circle using half the O.D.I. The Apex for the semi-circle will be where the horizontal and Center Line intersect. Trisect the semi-circle into 6 equal parts and label as shown. Draw lines up from the horizontal line to all labeled points on the arc. Add 3/4" to Line 1 for the bead and crimp allowance. Draw the Stretch-out as shown in Figure 2. Add a lap allowance.

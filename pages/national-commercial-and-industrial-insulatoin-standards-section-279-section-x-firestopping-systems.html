muca SECTION X FIRESTOPPING SYSTEMS 10.1 GENERAL firestopping shall consist of furnishing and installing a material or a combination of materials to form an effective barrier against the spread of flame, smoke, gases and moisture, It is to main- tain the integrity of the fire-resistance rated construction.

There are several sections for firestopping, Penetrations, Joints (Walltops/Boüoms, Expansion and Contraction) and Perimeter fire Containment.

However, this section encompasses only pen- Üations pertaining to insulated piping, ductwork and equipment.

10.2 CODES AND SPECIFICATIONS All firestopping shall be done in strict accordance with local build- ing code guidelines including but not limited to such code agen- Cies as: International Code Councill International Bulding Code, International fire Code.

and National Fire Protection Association 5000.

firestop systems must be installed according to manu- facturer•s specifications by qualified craftsmen.

There are many manufacturers of firestopping products, and each makes a broad range of products widi application specifications.

Be sure to check all product specifications prior to beginning any firestopping work.

10.3 FIRESTOPPING TERMINOLOGY There are terms and procedures which are industry specific to firestopping.

We have attempted to define such firestopping terms in the Glossary.

The glossary, though extensive, is not complete and any terms that are not understood should be reviewed before proceeding with any work.

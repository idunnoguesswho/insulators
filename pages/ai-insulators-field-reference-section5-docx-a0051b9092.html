Section 5: Material Specifications & Installation Standards

5.1 Overview

This section outlines materials, performance standards, and installation practices for insulation systems. All materials and assemblies shall comply with CSA, ASTM, and UL standards for safety, efficiency, and durability.

5.2 Thermal Properties & Material Selection

Selecting the right insulation material ensures performance under design temperature and service conditions. Common materials include fiberglass, mineral wool, foam glass, elastomeric rubber, and urethane foam.

5.4 Vapor Barriers & Weather Jacketing

Vapor barriers prevent condensation and corrosion. Weather jacketing protects insulation from mechanical damage and UV exposure.

Aluminum: 0.016–0.024 in thick, outdoor service.

Stainless Steel: for corrosive or high-impact environments.

PVC: economical indoor finish for cold systems.

ASJ (All-Service Jacket): foil-scrim-kraft vapor barrier for indoor use.

5.5 Firestopping Standards & Inspection Criteria

Firestopping ensures penetrations maintain rated fire resistance. Only listed and tested systems may be used.

ASTM E814 / UL 1479 – Through-penetration firestop systems.

ASTM E1966 / UL 2079 – Fire-resistance of joint systems.

ASTM E2174 – On-site inspection of installed firestops.

IFC 714 – Identification and labeling requirements.

Checklist:

Verify system identification and UL listing.

Ensure correct annular space and sealant materials.

Confirm visible labeling post-installation.

Figure 5-3: UL Firestop System Example [Colour retained]

5.6 Installation Practices & Quality Control

Cut insulation cleanly and square using sharp tools.

Apply adhesives evenly; avoid gaps and drips.

Maintain tolerances: ±3 mm circumference, ±6 mm length.

Record installation data (tag number, material, location).

5.7 Testing & Verification

Visual Inspection – confirm continuous coverage and sealed joints.

Adhesion Testing – perform periodic pull tests.

Thermal Testing – reference ASTM C680 for heat-loss calculations.

Label Verification – ensure each penetration has correct UL ID.

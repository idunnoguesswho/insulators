Section 66: Another Method for Gores to a Domed Head

PreviousNext

Required Information

  • Radius
  • Height

Formulas and Measurements

  • Draw a quarter circle in the bottom right area using the radius of the tank Divide the bottom right quarter circle into 12 equal parts and from these points set a straight edge that intersects the Apex to points on the small quarter circle.

Procedure

  1. Step 1

    Draw a horizontal line the exact length of the radius of the tank and the rise combined. Draw a 90 degree vertical line the exact length of the radius of the tank and rise combined. The two lines intersect as shown in the drawing

  2. Step 2

    Draw a quarter circle in the top left area using the rise as the radius. Draw a quarter circle in the bottom right area using the radius of the tank

  3. Step 3

    Divide the bottom right quarter circle into 12 equal parts and from these points set a straight edge that intersects the Apex to points on the small quarter circle. Mark all 11 points on the small quarter circle

  4. Step 4

    Draw 90 degree vertical lines up from every point on the large quarter circle as shown

  5. Step 5

    Draw 90 degree horizontal lines from every point of the small quarter circle as shown

  6. Step 6

    Label all points on both quarter circles as shown

  7. Step 7

    Where Lines 2 intersect, mark with a dot. Do the same for Lines 3 -12

  8. Step 8

    Connect all the dots to form the tank top curve

  9. Step 9

    Determine the gore width by the trial and error method, then draw half the gore width on vertical line 12 and from that point draw a line to the Apex

  10. Step 10

    Add a 1/2" bead and ccccrimp allowance to the initial horizontal line. Draw the Stretch-out in the regular way

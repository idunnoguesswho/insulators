Section 54: Two-piece 45 Degree Elbow by Rise

PreviousNext

Required Information

  • O.D.I.
  • Number of degrees
  • Rise

Formulas and Measurements

  • The Tangent of the Angle times Center Line Radius - Distance equals Rise.

Procedure

  1. Step 1

    Draw a horizontal line. Determine an Apex point to the right side and from the Apex, mark the Center Line Radius point which is 1 1/2 times the - Pipe Call-out size (all 45 degree Ells are considered to be Long Radius)

  2. Step 2

    Since this is a 45 degree elbow and a two-piece fitting, each angle is 22'/2 degrees. The Tangent of the Angle times Center Line Radius - Distance equals Rise. Calculate the rise and transfer that measurement to the Center Line. Draw a line from the Apex through the Rise point to a - point past the semi-circle

  3. Step 3

    Draw a semi-circle using half the O.D.I.. The Apex for the semi-circle will - be where the horizontal and Center Line intersect

  4. Step 4

    Trisect the semi-circle into 6 equal parts and label as shown

  5. Step 5

    Draw lines up from the horizontal line to all labeled points on the arc

  6. Step 6

    Add 3/4" to Line 1 for the bead and ccccrimp allowance

  7. Step 7

    Draw the Stretch-out as shown in Figure 2. Add a lap allowance. Center Line Radius

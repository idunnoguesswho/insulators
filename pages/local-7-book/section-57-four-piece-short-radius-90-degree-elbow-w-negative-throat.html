Procedure

  1. Step 1

    Draw a 90 degree angle. With a short radius fitting multiply the pipe call-out size times 1 to obtain the Center Line Radius

  2. Step 2

    Draw this Center Line Radius and on each side of the Center Line draw an arc showing the actual pipe size

  3. Step 3

    Next add the insulation lines (when drawing the insulation lines, notice that the throat is outside the Apex; this is a Negative Throat Fitting.) Label Point A on the vertical line

  4. Step 4

    Bisect the 90 degree arc. Then using a set from the Apex to the outside arc line, set dividers at Point A and mark a Point B on the outside arc. Mark a set of divider points from the bisection line to Point B and mark a point on the outside arc to form Point C. Draw a line from the Apex to Point C; extend a little beyond the point

  5. Step 5

    Set dividers from the Center Line (where the horizontal and Center Lines intersect) to the O.D.I. line and draw a semi-circle. Trisect the semi-circle into six equal parts and label as shown

  6. Step 6

    Add 3/8" to the Heel and draw this line to the Apex

  7. Step 7

    For the Stretch-out, draw a line the length of the circumference and divide that line into 12 equal parts and label as shown

  8. Step 8

    Take measurements from Figurel and transfer to the Stretch-out as shown in Figure 2 to the corresponding numbered lines. Mark the Points 4 for the start and finish points for the bead and ccccrimp

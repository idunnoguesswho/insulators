Section 74: Taper with a 90 Degree Round Run-out

PreviousNext

Required Information

  • Height
  • Large diameter
  • Small diameter
  • True height

Procedure

  1. Step 1

    Draw an Elevation View of the fitting

  2. Step 2

    Draw the Center Lines for both the Taper and the Run-out so that they intersect

  3. Step 3

    Draw a semi-circle at the end of the run-out and trisect that semi-circle. Draw lines at 90 degrees from these points to the Center Line and label the points on semi-circle as shown

  4. Step 4

    Where the top and bottom lines of the run-out intersect the taper, draw perpendicular lines up and down as shown

  5. Step 5

    Swing quarter circle arcs, using the radius of the run-out, from the points where the top and bottom lines of the run-out enter the taper. These quarter circles will be drawn to the perpendicular lines in

  6. Step 6

    Draw another two quarter circles to represent the radii of both large and small diameters. These will be drawn using the Center Line as an Apex point

  7. Step 7

    Trisect the two first quarter circles drawn in

  8. Step 8

    Where these lines intersect both larger arcs, draw 90 degree lines up and down to the outside lines of the run-out

  9. Step 9

    Connect corresponding lines from both outside lines of the run- out and where they intersect with the run-out lines, place a dot and connect all those dots

  10. Step 10

    The Stretch-out will be drawn as shown in

  11. Step 11

    Draw the body as a Taper would be drawn. Draw the hole cut- out as was shown in previous layouts

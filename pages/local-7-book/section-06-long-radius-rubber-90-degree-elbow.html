Section 6: Long Radius Rubber 90 Degree Elbow

PreviousNext

Required Information

  • Pipe Call-out Size
  • Thickness of Insulation

Formulas and Measurements

  • This is the Center Line Radius Divide the Pipe Call-out Size by 2 and measure from the Center Line Radius towards the Apex, both vertically and horizontally, to create Points 1 & 2 and label them Set dividers at Points 1& 2 and swing an arc from the Apex to form Cut-line A Multiply the Pipe Call-out Size by Pi, divide by 2, and add 1 1/2.
  • Use this measurement to mark a point on the vertical line from Cut-line A Set dividers at the Apex to this point and swing an arc to the horizontal line to form Cut-line B Cut-line C is created by drawing a 22 1/2 degree line from the Apex to the outside arc.

Procedure

  1. Step 1

    Draw a 90 degree angle; from the Apex swing an arc using the Pipe Call-out Size times 1 1/2. This is the Center Line Radius

  2. Step 2

    Divide the Pipe Call-out Size by 2 and measure from the Center Line Radius towards the Apex, both vertically and horizontally, to create Points 1 & 2 and label them

  3. Step 3

    Set dividers at Points 1& 2 and swing an arc from the Apex to form Cut-line A

  4. Step 4

    Multiply the Pipe Call-out Size by Pi, divide by 2, and add 1 1/2. Use this measurement to mark a point on the vertical line from Cut-line A

  5. Step 5

    Set dividers at the Apex to this point and swing an arc to the horizontal line to form Cut-line B

  6. Step 6

    Cut-line C is created by drawing a 22 1/2 degree line from the Apex to the outside arc.

Procedure

  1. Step 1

    Draw a Base Line with a 90 degree vertical line at each end. Plan enough room to draw the rest of the layout

  2. Step 2

    Using half the O.D.I. of the large diameter insulation, draw a 90 degree arc on the left vertical line as shown in Figurel. Do the same for the smaller O.D.I. on the right vertical line

  3. Step 3

    Draw a 90 degree vertical line between the 2 arcs and label it A

  4. Step 4

    Trisect or bisect the small arc into equal parts (in this case three)

  5. Step 5

    Draw parallel horizontal lines from the right bisected or trisected points on the 90 degree lines down to the Base

  6. Step 6

    Label the vertical Lines 1 to 4 starting at the Base Line, and extend those lines to the points on the small arc as shown in

  7. Step 7

    Divide half the circumference Into six equal parts and draw Line A at 90 degrees to vertical lines for the Stretch-out

  8. Step 8

    From Figure 3 set dividers on the Line A and Line 1 on the large arc. Transfer this measurement to Line 1 on your Stretch-out. Do the same for the rest of the Stretch-out, by advancing to the next number up each time. For the other half of the fitting add a lap

  9. Step 9

    For the body portion of the fitting, cut a piece of material half the circumference of the large O.D.I. and draw a Center Line as in

  10. Step 10

    From Figure 3 set dividers at Point 1 and Point 2 on the large arc and transfer this measurement to the Stretch-out as shown in Figure 3. Do the same, but stepping up from 2 to 3, then 3 to 4. Draw 90 degree lines on both sides of the Center Line through the points obtained and label those lines 1, 2, 3, 8 4 as in

  11. Step 11

    From Figure 3 set dividers at the Base Line and Point 4 on the large arc. Transfer this measurement to Line 4 on the Stretch-out in Figure 3. Do the same for 3 and 2. 1 is just to a point. Connect all the points to obtain half the hole cut-out for the other half of the fitting. Add for a lap. In some instances tabs may be required to lock the stem to the body

Preamble

The following working rules are adopted by the Members of Local 110 of the International Association of

Heat and Frost Insulators and Allied Workers, with the most recent changes having been made at the

meeting on January 22, 2026.

Whereas, most improvements in working conditions for working people in Alberta were accomplished

by the efforts of organized labour, and

Whereas the welfare of the Members of a Union can best be protected and advanced by their united

action, therefore, we have organized this Local Union. It is our own creation, time, money and labour

has made it what it is, an organization to be proud of. Officers and Staff must be respected, Committees

must attend to their duties; assessments, fines and dues must be paid promptly. Read your Bylaws and

remember them; ignorance of them will not be an excuse for their violation.

Any portion of these Bylaws when conflicting with new federal or provincial legislation, or by the signing

of a Local Union Collective Agreement or as to conflict with the Constitution and Bylaws of the

International Association of Heat and Frost Insulators and Allied Workers will be invalid and if need be

amended by action of the Executive Board.

Section 1 – Effective Date of the Local 110 Bylaws

The Working Rules and Bylaws of Local 110 shall become effective on the date of adoption by the

Membership, and shall continue in full force and effect until such time as a meeting is convened to

amend or terminate them.

Section 2 – Local 110 Meetings

2.1 The Executive Board shall meet on the Third Tuesday of each month starting at 7:30 PM, running

no longer than three (3) hours. All business past the three (3) hour mark will be held over until

the next month under Unfinished Business.

2.2 The Local 110 General Membership monthly meeting is to be held the Third Wednesday of each

month starting at 7:30 PM, running no longer than three (3) hours. All business past the three

(3) hour mark will be held over until the next month under Unfinished Business.

2.3 General Membership Meetings will be hosted at the Local 110 Edmonton Union Hall and

Electronically attended through closed circuit teleconference at the Local 110 Calgary Union

Hall. Once a location is obtained Members will be able to attend in the Fort McMurray area

through closed circuit teleconference.

2.4 At the discretion of the Business Manager, Business Agent(s), and Executive Board, bi-monthly

or monthly Information Meetings shall be held when and where deemed necessary due to the

size of the workforce and/or the hours of work relative to the area outside of the Edmonton,

Calgary, and Fort McMurray work areas.

2.5 Attendance at Information Meetings will apply towards eligibility for elections.

2.6 In the event that a major agenda item is scheduled for a General Membership Meeting (such as

changes in finances e.g., changes to dues), the Business Manager, President or Business Agent

will ensure a message is sent by blast email, added to the Local 110 website, or by mail as

appropriate. This message must be available to Members on the Local 110 website at a

minimum on the night before the General Membership Meeting. If such an item is known to be

on the agenda earlier, the Business Manager, President or Business Agent will ensure a message

is on the Local 110 website, seven (7) days before the general membership meeting.

Section 3 – Local Officers Election Procedures

3.1 Local 110 will elect for four (4) year terms the following Officers; President, Vice- President,

Business Manager (combined with the Financial Secretary and Corresponding Secretary), 3

Business Agents (1 Southern Alberta, 1 Central Alberta and 1 Northern Alberta), 3 Local Union

Trustees, 5 Executive Board Members, 2 Sergeant at arms (1 Edmonton and 1 Calgary), and 1

Recording Secretary.

3.2 The Local will retain a neutral third party prior to the Notice of Nomination being sent to the

Membership. The duties of the third party will be to collect all mail in ballots and confirm

eligibility as per the list provided by the Election Committee Chair. To bring the ballots for the

count, observe the count and provide final results to the Election Committee Chair, President

and Business Manager. First right of refusal for this position will be given to the appointed

Auditor for Local 110.

3.3 Local 110 President will appoint a five (5) Member Election Committee on or before the date of

the Nominations meeting. The Committee’s first order of business will be to elect the

Committee Chair.

3.4 Nominations will be held at the November General Membership meeting in the final year of the

term.

3.5 Notice of Nomination meeting and Election will be as per the International Constitution and

Bylaws Article XX.

3.6 Due to the extremely large geographical area of Local 110 the Local will utilize the mail in ballot

system. Additionally, the Local 110 office in Edmonton will be open from 09:00 till 15:00 the last

Friday before the Election Committee count, to allow Members to cast an in-person ballot. This

will be manned by Members of the Election Committee and if two ballots are cast by the same

Member both will be removed from the count.

3.7 In the case of the President, Vice- President, Business Manager or Business Agent(s) vacating

their position prior to the end of their set term, a mail in ballot process will be repeated. For any

other position, Nomination and Election by secret ballot vote will be conducted as part of the

Local 110 monthly General Membership Meeting.

3.8 No member shall simultaneously hold an elected or appointed officer position in Local 110 and

an elected or appointed officer position in any other labour union.

Section 4 – Delegate Election Procedures

In situations that Local 110 will be sending delegates to Conferences or other events over and above the

automatic delegates due to elected position, as stated in the Constitution and Bylaws of the

International Association of Heat and Frost Insulators and Allied Workers:

4.1 Local 110 President will appoint a Three (3) member Election Committee on or before the date

of the Nominations meeting. The Committees first order of business will be to elect the

Committee Chair.

4.2 Notice of Nomination and Election will be as per the International Constitution and By-Laws

Article XX.

4.3 Nomination and Election by secret ballot vote will be conducted as part of the Local 110

monthly General Membership Meeting.

Section 5 - Duties of the Executive Board

5.1 The Executive Board is charged with carrying out those duties and obligations outlined in Article

XXI, Section 11 of the International Constitution & Bylaws.

5.2 Local 110 Executive Board will sit as the Trial Board for all member charges under the

International Constitution and Bylaws and the Local 110 Bylaws and Code of Conduct.

5.3 The President, Vice President, 5 Executive Board Members, Sergeant at arms and Recording

Secretary must attend the Executive Board meeting and the General Membership Meeting to be

eligible for monetary payment that has been properly motioned and passed at General

Membership Meeting.

5.4 Executive Board Members will have the option to attend Executive Board Meetings through

Section 6 – Seeking Employment

6.1 Any Members whose employment ends can phone the Union office in Edmonton or in Calgary to

have their name affixed to the unemployed list. They will be given a verification number for

future reference. Members may also affix their name to the unemployed list through their

online profile. Members names will be affixed to the unemployment list the time and date they

sign, call in, or use their online profile after their termination of employment.

6.2 The offices in Edmonton and Calgary are open for business to the Membership from 8:00 a.m. to

12:00 noon and 1:00 p.m. until 4:30 p.m., Monday through Friday (excluding statutory holidays).

6.3 Members must notify Local 110, within fifteen (15) working days, of a change in mailing address

or telephone number. Failure to do so as evidenced by repeatedly returned mail or documented

attempts of contacting an invalid telephone number, would lead to the union setting a fine

which will double for repeat offenses, after a hearing before the Executive Board into the details

of the charge.

6.4 Members wishing to work in another Local’s territory must obtain a Travel Card as outlined in

the International Constitution and Bylaws Article XVII and shall have their name remain on the

unemployed list and move up in normal fashion.

6.5 Local will maintain two unemployed list systems. One list for the dispatch of Members to

designated Commercial projects and one for the dispatch of Members to all other projects.

6.6 Members will have the option to sign both unemployed lists, but when dispatched will be

Removed from both unemployed lists. If the Member is signed only to the Commercial

Unemployed list and is dispatched to a noncommercial project the difference in nonworking

dues will be paid prior to dispatch.

Section 7 - Dispatch / Referrals

7.1 Dispatch will commence at 9:00 a.m. and end at 3:00 p.m., Monday through Friday (exceptions

will be emergency calls). Members who bid in person at the union halls or by phone between

8:00 am and 9:00 am or through the online dispatch system between 12:00 am to 9:00 am are

eligible to be dispatched starting at 9:00am. Any Members, or Travelers who request a dispatch

after 9:00 am will be dispatched after the Members, or Travelers, who called in prior to 9:00 am.

7.2 Any Member who takes a job call in person, by telephone, or by online bidding commits the

dispatcher to hold a referral, and then does not report to work or honour that referral, will be

subject to charges.

A violation of this section will remain on the Member’s record but after 36 months the next

violation will be fined at the level of a first violation provided that:

a) the Member is in good standing during the 36 months from the date of the initial

violation; and

b) the Member has no other violations of Local 110’s Bylaws and Code of Conduct, or the

International Constitution and Bylaws, during the 36 months from the date of the initial

violation.

7.3 Referrals will normally be issued in the order by which a Member’s name appears on the

unemployed list should they indicate they want the job or by name hire once their acceptance

has been confirmed by the Dispatcher.

7.4 Employees shall be classified by the Union (on their referrals) and cannot work for less than

their referral classification.

7.5 Members shall personally pick up their referral at the Union office, or have it emailed or sent

electronically to them self and the contractor, unless other arrangements have been agreed to

with the Business Manager and/or Agent.

7.6 All Members who commit to a call with required safety training must complete the required

training prior to reporting to the project unless it is available on the project.

7.7 Local 110 will leave name hires visible on work orders posted at the Local 110 website.

7.8 Members who are legitimately laid off from a project due to medical issues can be recalled to

the contractor once the Member is medically cleared for dispatch. However, a new work order

from the Contractor must be placed with the Union Hall, and a new referral is needed, before

the Member returns to work.

7.09 All Members will notify the Union office when being transferred from one industrial project to

another and a new referral must be issued.

7.10 All Members must have a VALID dispatch slip to work on any project and retain a signed copy of

this referral during the course of employment.

7.11 On projects where asbestos removal work is being done or will be done, Members with

Section 8 – Incoming Travel Card Rules

8.1 Any Travel Card Member who has deposited a Travel Card at the Local 110 hall, and is working in

the area, when requested by the Business Manager or Business Agent to leave the area, shall do

so immediately. Any Traveler not complying will be subject to charges, and removed from

eligibility for dispatch through Local 110.

Section 9 – Dispatching Local 110 Travelers to Other Locals

9.1 When another Insulator Local Union contacts Local 110 and requests Local 110 Members to fill

posted jobs in their territory these calls will be posted at the Local 110 Edmonton Union Hall and

if no outstanding calls are on the Local 110 dispatch board the call will be posted to the dispatch

website.

9.2 Dispatch of these workers will be conducted as per the regular dispatch rules outlined in these

by-laws.

9.3 No Local 110 Member is restricted from approaching another Insulator Local Union on their own

looking for a job.

9.4 Travel Cards will only be issued when the Member is in compliance with Article XVII of the

International Constitution and Bylaws and there is no work available to the Member in Local

110’s territory.

Section 10 – Job Stewards

10.1 It is the responsibility of every Member to inform the Union office if there is no Job Steward on

the project they are working on (if this is not done each Member on that site is subject to being

charged).

10.2 Job Stewards will be appointed on all projects by the Business Manager or Business Agent.

(They will make no official decisions but will report any conditions to the Business Manager and

Agent.) They have the authority, with the advice and the consent of the Business Manager and

Page 9

8

Business Agent(s) in each instance, to investigate in the adjudication of grievances on their

jobsite.

10.3 Job Stewards must be Journeyman Members of the Local Union in good standing, and no

Foreman shall be a Job Steward.

10.4 The Local Union shall provide an appropriate Job Steward Kit to appointed Job Stewards.

10.5 All Job Stewards or acting Job Stewards shall wear identification supplied by

the Local Union.

10.6 Any complaint to the Job Steward shall be submitted in writing and signed by the complainant.

10.7 Any Member or Members found guilty of intimidation of the Job Steward in the performance of

his/her duties, or any Member refusing to assist the Job Steward in the performance of his/her

duties, will be subject to charges.

10.8 Job Steward must notify the Local Union office as well as the Business Agent of the area if they

are no longer able to complete their duties and return all documentation or union equipment to

the Local or next appointed Job Steward.

10.9 The duties of a Job Steward will include:

a) To have in his possession a copy of the International Constitution and Bylaws of Local

110 and a copy of the current Collective Agreement for the project.

b) Encourage Union Membership and labour solidarity.

c) To check all Employees for possession of a valid signed dispatch slip, credentials, and

required Member supplied tools.

d) To report to the Business Manager or Business Agent any encroachment upon the

Local’s jurisdiction of work, any violation of the Local’s working rules and bylaws or of

the International Constitution, or any violation of the Collective Agreement and/or of

the Local 110 Code of Conduct.

e) Any violation concerning occupational health and safety legislation shall also be

reported to the Union office.

f) To complete any applicable Job Steward Training Course(s), as determined by the

Executive Board and as may be amended from time to time.

Section 11 – Work Stoppages

11.1 Under no circumstances shall a Job Steward or Member of the Local cause a stoppage of work.

In the case of any dispute over the interpretation, application or administration of a Collective

Agreement, the Local working rules, or the International Constitution; such disputes shall be

reported immediately to the Business Manager or Business Agent, who will deal with them.

Section 12 – Apprenticeship

12.1 All Members shall comply with the current Apprenticeship Regulations (Any Members who has

his/her apprenticeship cancelled is subject to being lapsed from the Union ninety (90) days after

such cancellation has occurred).

12.2 An Apprentice who joins the Union as a Registered Apprentice with the Alberta Government

Apprenticeship Board must advance their apprenticeship by regularly attending the technical

training that is required in order for the Contract of Apprenticeship to be completed. An

Apprentice who fails to do so by failing to register for classes, canceling registration or not

successfully passing required Alberta apprenticeship exam, will be required to appear before the

Executive Board to explain their reasons for their failure to comply, and may be subject to a

penalty to be determined by the President.

12.3 Apprentices while attending Apprenticeship Trade School, can sign the Union Unemployed list,

and will then pay non-working dues while attending school. However, a new work order from

the Contractor must be placed with the Union Hall, and a new referral is needed, before the

Apprentice returns to work. If the Apprentice does not sign the Unemployed book while in

Apprenticeship Trade School, the Apprentice shall pay full working dues for the time the

Apprentice. attends training.

Section 13 – Apprentice Duties

13.1 Apprentice’s duties are to assist all Journeyman in any way possible. All Apprentices,

wherever possible, shall comply with and be under the supervision of a Journeyman at all times.

13.2 Apprentices who are uncooperative with Journeyman will be reported to the Job

Steward. After a Journeyman has registered a complaint against the Apprentice, the Job

Steward shall report this Apprentice to the Business Manager or the Business Agent(s), who will

in turn investigate and report findings to the Executive Board. The Executive Board will then call

the Apprentice to appear before them so that the Executive Board may determine appropriate

disciplinary measures.

13.3 Any Apprentice who wishes to be upgraded to a higher classification (e.g. from first year to

second year) must present the Apprentice Blue Book with the appropriate stamp to the Union

Hall. Any Apprentice who does not present the Apprentice Blue Book with the appropriate

stamp will not be upgraded to a higher classification.

13.4 Any Apprentice who is upgrading from Apprentice to Journeyman must provide a copy of the

Section 14 – Work Rules

14.1 No Member shall do piece work or labour contracting of any kind.

14.2 Banking of hours will only be allowed on Projects where it is allowed through a specific

Collective Agreement or agreed to by the Employer and Local 110.

14.3 No Member or Members shall enter into an agreement with their Employer on any subject

which is dealt with in the Local Union Agreements. Any Member found doing so shall be subject

to charges.

14.4 Any Member who counsels or coerces another Member into performing unsafe work is guilty of

an offense against the Local Union and is subject to charges.

Section 15 – Strike Breakers

15.1 Members that are found to be employed as strike breakers or who violate a legal picket line are

subject to charges.

Section 16 - Wearing of Coveralls

16.1 All Members will wear full sleeve coveralls while working with or handling

any insulation materials and their protective coverings. They should encourage other trades

workers to do so as well. Coveralls shall be kept in good repair to prevent insulation materials

from contaminating clothing worn underneath.

16.2 All Members will remove coveralls in designated change room facilities prior

to lunch or coffee breaks and have their lunch or coffee in a separate designated place

(designated by the employer).

16.3 All clothing which is exposed outside of coveralls (such as the hoods of hooded sweaters) will be

removed along with the coveralls and left in the change room.

16.4 Clothing worn to work will be clean and in good repair and shall be appropriate to the

workplace.

Section 17 – Organizing

17.1 A Member may be authorized to take a non-union job insulating on condition that they agree to

abide by an agreement they must sign with the Business Manager or their designate prior to

commencing such employment.

17.2 All Members working under organizing agreements will be required to complete reports

as provided by Local 110.

17.3 All Members working under organizing agreements will pay the Local the minimum weekly

Non-Working dues. Change to be made as per direction of GP Revard

Section 18 – Membership

18.1 Membership Application and Fees

All applications for membership in Local 110 shall be submitted to the Executive Board for

review and decision. The general membership shall not vote on new member applications,

except for members transferring from another local, who shall be voted on at a General

Membership Meeting.

Applications must be received at least one (1) week prior to the scheduled monthly meeting

(Executive Board for new applicants; General Membership for transfers).

Applicants shall serve as Permit Workers until their membership application is approved by the

Executive Board and all membership initiation fees have been paid in full.

During the permit period, applicants must:

Recommendation of three hundred fifty (350) hours of work under Local 110 jurisdiction; and

Obtain three (3) references/signatures from Journeyman members as part of the application

process.

Members wishing to transfer from another local to Local 110 must provide three (3) references

from either their home local or Local 110 Journeyman members.

Upon acceptance, the applicant shall pay an initiation fee of fifty dollars ($50).

18.2 Orientation Requirement

All new members, including transfers, must complete New Member Orientation and be sworn in

18.3 New Member Orientation will be held bi-monthly either in person or online.

Section 19 - Union Dues, Active Members/Withdrawal Card Members

19.1 The Local will only utilize dues money collected after August 1, 2022, for “Core” activities of the

Local, as prescribed by the Government of Alberta.

19.2 Industrial/Hazardous Material Handler Members will pay a base due of $8.00 per week, plus an

additional three percent (3%) of gross wage when working through the Local.

19.3 Commercial/Firestop/Production Worker Members will pay a base due of $4.00 per week, plus

an additional three percent (3%) of gross wage when working through the Local.

19.4 Local 110 Members or prospective Members who are employed with a given insulation

contractor at the time they are certified by Local 110, will not be required to pay any working

union dues to Local 110 when working for that company for upwards to six months following

the date it was certified.

19.5 Members who are in arrears in their union dues are subject to a fifty dollar ($50.00) fine if they

do not pay up to date or produce receipts within the prescribed period after being requested to

do so by the Union office.

19.6 A Member who becomes ill for a period of more than one week, and who provides relevant

medical documentation, can sign the unemployed book, pay working dues for the first week of

time off work due to illness, and then remain for up to six (6) months without paying further

dues subject to providing medical documentation of continuing illness to the Union Hall. If, after

six (6) months, the Member is still unable to return to work, the Member will pay non-working

dues until the Member receives a new referral from the Union Hall. The Member MUST obtain a

“return to work from medical” referral from the Union Hall to be considered in possession of a

valid dispatch slip.

Section 20 – Mortuary Fund

20.1 Effective from the date a Member completes New Member Orientation, that Member’s

beneficiary shall thereafter be eligible to receive a death benefit from the Union’s Mortuary

Fund in the event of such member’s death, subject to having paid all Mortuary Assessments

being paid in full.

20.2 The Executive Board may review and set the base amount of monies to be paid out to

beneficiaries (not more often than once every twelve months). All beneficiaries will receive the

same base amount of payment following a change being made.

20.3 At the end of each fiscal year as part of the tax package, the union shall send a letter to each

Member’s last given mailing address, assessing them ten dollars ($10.00) for each Member who

has passed away within the previous year.

20.4 Full payment of the assessment will be required on or before April 30th of that year to continue

coverage for that year. Coverage will be from May 1st to April 30th.

20.5 If full payment of the assessment is not made by April 30th the Member’s beneficiary shall not

be eligible for death benefits if the Member should pass away before the assessment is paid in

full.

20.6 After two (2) years of nonpayment of the Mortuary Fund assessment the Member will be lapsed

from the fund. Such Members will no longer be assessed for mortuary but will remain Members

in good standing, provided they meet the other applicable obligations as called for by the Local

110 Bylaws and/or International Constitution and Bylaws.

20.7 Once an active Member is lapsed from the Mortuary Fund, in order to re-gain benefits the

Member must pay all prior missed Mortuary Fund assessments, all outstanding fines, and a two-

hundred-dollar ($200.00) administration fee.

20.8 Once an Honorary Card member or Grey Card member or Withdrawal Card member has lost

their eligibility with the Mortuary Fund, they will only re-gain eligibility for death benefits if that

Member again works at the trade through Local 110 for a minimum of 480 hours.

20.9 Any Member who was lapsed from Local 110 and is seeking re-affiliation must pay any

outstanding Mortuary Assessments that were unpaid at the time of their lapsing.

Section 22 – Failing to Comply with Local Bylaws

22.1 A Member who fails to comply with any of these working rules and bylaws subjects themselves

to an assessment of penalty after a Hearing by the Executive Board, sitting as the Trial Board.

Section 23 – Quality of Work

23.1 All workers dispatched from Local 110 are expected to at all times meet and maintain the

generally accepted standards for quality of work in the Insulator Trade and in the Insulation

Industry. Demonstrating an inability to do so will prompt remedial steps as prescribed in the

International Association of Heat and Frost Insulators and Allied Workers Constitution and

Bylaws.

Section 24 – Amend or Terminate Bylaws

24.1 These bylaws shall remain in effect for at least one year from the date when a Special Called

Meeting was held to adopt all of them, unless the entire Executive Board, at an Executive Board

meeting, vote that it is necessary to alter, delete or create a bylaw before one year has elapsed

since the last Special Called Meeting where the bylaws were adopted.

24.2 If as above, the Executive Board do vote to alter, delete, or create a bylaw; they must then bring

the proposed change to a General Membership Meeting or a Special Called Meeting, to be voted

on the Journeyman Members in attendance after notice of motion is sent by mail to the entire

Journeyman Membership (24 days’ notice).

24.3 If the Membership at the General Membership Meeting or Special Called Meeting agree, by

majority vote, with the proposed change by the Executive Board, the proposed change will then

take effect from that date forward.

24.4 Any such meeting, once agreed to, will require at least 28 days’ notice to be given to the

Journeyman Membership by mail. In this same notice, the Membership will be advised that any

proposed changes they might want voted on at the Special Called Meeting, must be presented

to the Corresponding Secretary at least fifteen business days prior to the meeting, so that the

Executive Board have time to review and make recommendations concerning them.

24.5 At the Special Called Meeting, any Mechanic Member can propose to alter, delete, or create a

bylaw if:

Local 110 Code of Conduct

Preamble

All human beings are born free and equal in dignity and rights.

They are endowed with reason and conscience and should act towards one another in the spirit of

brotherhood and sisterhood.

Under the Canada Labour Code, every employee is entitled to employment free of harassment and

without discrimination.

Action

This Code of Conduct will be adhered to by all Members of Local 110, and all Travelers who are referred

to work by Local 110.

This Code of Conduct is considered to be a part of the Local 110 Working Rules and Bylaws (as amended

from time to time), and violating any aspect of this Code of Conduct will be considered as a violation of

the Local 110 Working Rules and Bylaws, subject to penalty such as determined by the Local 110

Executive Board and President after a Hearing is conducted to consider any such alleged violations.

This Code of Conduct shall apply to:

a. Camps

b. Job site workplaces

c. Job site lunchrooms/coffee rooms/change rooms

d. Temporary accommodation for those being paid subsistence pay

e. Union meetings/scheduled social events

f. Union office premises

g. All interactions, including Email and Phone communication, with Contractor Employees

h. All interactions, including Email and Phone communication, with Staff and Officers of

the Local

This Code of Conduct acknowledges that discrimination, whether intentional or unintentional, is unfair,

differential treatment of individuals and groups based on prejudice, ignorance, fear, or stereotypes.

Discrimination imposes burdens on, or denies opportunities to, individuals or groups and is unfair

because it is not based on actual academic or job performance, or any other form of competence.

Instead, it is based on the erroneous assumption that a particular individual shares attributes, usually

negative, stereotypically associated with a group to which he or she is perceived to belong.

Discriminating on any of the following 13 grounds or other such grounds that may arise violates Local

110’s Policy on Discrimination and Harassment. Conduct of any Member of the Union amounting to

discrimination or harassment of another individual by that Member within the 13 grounds below or on

any other such grounds which may arise that cause humiliation or intimidation which has the effect of

excluding and/or isolating an individual or group shall constitute discrimination or harassment.

- Age

- Ancestry

- Colour

- Family status

- Marital status

- Physical and/or mental disability

- Place of origin

- Political belief

- Race

- Religion

- Sex (including gender and pregnancy)

- Sexual orientation / Gender identity

- Unrelated criminal conviction

Harassment (a form of discrimination) is comments, conduct or behaviour that humiliates, intimidates,

excludes and/or isolates an individual or group based on the 13 grounds of prohibited discrimination

above or such other grounds as may arise. Harassment is unwanted or unwelcome attention from a

person who knows, or ought to know, that behaviour is unwelcome. Harassment can include written or

Section 26 – Financial Matters

26.1 The combined balance of all regular operating accounts shall not exceed one-half (½) of the

most current fiscal year’s audited operating expenses.

26.2 Any funds in excess of the limit set out in 26.1 shall be invested, at the discretion of the Financial

Secretary, with a minimum of two (2), or up to a maximum of four (4) investment management

firms currently managing funds within the Local 110 Pension Plan.

26.3 Transfers of funds made under Section 26 shall occur no less than once per fiscal year.

Transferred funds to investment managers shall be evenly distributed among the chosen firms

at the time of investment.

26.4 The Financial Secretary shall not be required to redistribute investment earnings to maintain an

equal balance among firms.

26.5 In the event that the Union’s operating account holdings fall below one-quarter (¼) of the

previous year’s audited operating expenses, the Financial Secretary may withdraw funds from

an investment manager of their choosing to a limit of one-quarter (1/4) of the previous year’s

audited net operating expenses.

26.6 All investments and withdrawals made under Section 26 shall be consistent with the

International Constitution and Bylaws and reported to the Executive Board.

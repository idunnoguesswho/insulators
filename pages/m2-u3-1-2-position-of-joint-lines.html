1.2 Position of Joint lines

We always aim to put the joint line on the shortest side. The reason it is put there is to save time and effort. So metimes we put the joint elsewhere for appearances of a job or to maximise use of metal. On the development shown above the joint is positioned on the line A-A as this is the shortest side of the pattern. There are many joints in sheet metal. So me joints are called self-secured joints. This is where we use the job’s own metal. Examples are Groove joint, Slip joint and Peined joint. They all have different amounts of metal involved in their assembly. It is important to allow for these joints when marking out a pattern.

muca SECTION IV GENERAL APPLICATION METHODS/ILLUSTRATIONS 4.1 INTRODUCTION TO THE PLATES The illustrations in this section represent applications deemed acceptable by members of the insulation industry.

They illustrate the application of a certain form (i.e.

pipe insulation, blanket) rather than type (i.e.

cellular glass, mineral fiber) of insulation, on particular installations.

Several alternative application methods and material choices are represented for each type of installation.

For example, elbows may be insulated using either a pre-formed elbow.

mitered segments of insulation, or a PVC/glass fiber elbow system.

The combinations of insulation materials, finishes and secure- ments illustrated in the plates should not be interpreted as prescriptive or recommended installations.

Although the insulation systems illustrated represent correct practices and compatible materials choices, they do not represent the only acceptable combinations.

In an attempt to cover as many of the design conditions possible in any one installation, many illustrations depict a more complete application than is neces- sary.

For instance, concealed installations often do not require finishes or securements as shown on some of the plates.

These choices are dependent on several design criteria more clearly discussed in Sections II and III.

The purpose of Section IV is to visualize for the specifier, installer and inspector the correct application of materials on specific installations.

A specifying engineer may use the illustra- tions to communicate to the contractor, installer or inspector how the completed installation should appear, eliminating potential misunderstandings or confusion.

He should correspond his list of materials, thicknesses and application notes to the numbers on the illustration.

The contractor may use the drawings in a similar manner when preparing submittals, proposals or when communicating instructions to installation personnel.

GENERAL APPLICATION Application of pre-formed pipe insulation may be in single or multiple layers.

In the majority of installations, single layer construction is used.

However, multiple layer construction may be required on large sized pipes, long runs of piping or piping at extreme low or high temperatures.

Joints should be staggered and the layers properly secured.

See Plate No.

1.

Pipe insulation is available in many forms.

Jacketing is either factory-applied or field applied.

Variations in application method occur mainly due to the type of finish and support/hanger used, rather than the generic make-up of the pipe insulation itself.

COLD PIPING Vapor-retarder design is a major consideration on low temperature and cold piping.

In order to isolate vapor- retarder failures on low temperature piping, it is sometimes necessary to apply a vapor seal to butt joints at specified points along the line.

Multiple layer construction is often necessary on low temperature and refrigerated piping.

For extreme low temperature applica- tions see Section V, Plates No.

(C) To achieve thickness in excess of manufacturers' capabilities.

(D) For retrofitting purposes.

To obtain optimum insulation value at joints, each layer is stag- gered when applied.

Each layer must be secured in place before the next is applied.

8 PIPING Traced Piping Tracers provide heat for piping which must be protected from freezing, and for process piping which must be maintained at a minimum temperature.

The two most common types are (1) tubing which runs horizontally along the length of the piping, and (2) electrical cables or tape coiled around the pipe.

Tracers may be thermally connected to the pipe by means of heat transfer cement, or simply banded to the pipe.

The exact type and length of tracing should be indicated in the specification as it may affect the size of pipe insulation neces- sary and/or the application time which must be estimated.

The insulation material chosen should insulate efficiently at the temperature of the tracer as well as that of the pipe.

Jacketing materials must be weather-proof if tracing is located outdoors.

Plate No.

Other applications include the use of wrap insulations and specially made extended leg pipe insulations.

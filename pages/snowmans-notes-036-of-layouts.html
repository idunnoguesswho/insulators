2.6 Efficient Drawing

Layout and Sequencing

of Layouts

Today it is customary in the

insulating industry to develop

fittings according to section

2.5 above. When cutting the

plates to size, metal waste and

cutting can be reduced by

alternatively laying the various

segments one under the other.

It is very important to nest the

various patterns together to

maximise economy of

materials. Also the connection

of the individual segments is

almost only made with a

beaded piece and counter

piece as shown under the arc

in figure 1. The average

allowance for the beaded

pieces is 8 mm, in other words

4 mm for half the

development. For smaller

pipes and small beaded pieces

3mm is enough of an

allowance.
